Summary: Freedom2hear is a platform built to make online communities safer and more inclusive. I'm responsible for designing, building, iterating, and managing their marketing website in Webflow.

## Backstory

Over roughly two years, I led multiple iterations of the Marketing website, using data and user behaviour to guide each evolution. The entire site — including CMS, marketing pages, and blog — is built and managed in Webflow, and I worked closely with both a creative agency and a fractional CMO to align brand, product, and go-to-market strategy.

---

## Contributions

I delivered the brand transition from GoBubble to Freedom2hear, ensuring the new identity was consistently applied across marketing assets, sales materials, and digital touchpoints.

I built and maintained the Webflow site structure, templates, and CMS, and implemented Google Analytics and Hotjar to track key events. Using these insights, I redesigned the site navigation — including a new flyout design — rethinking the information architecture to guide users more effectively to high-value pages.

Working closely with a Digital Marketing Consultant, I ensured the site structure, page architecture, and content supported broader SEO and traffic acquisition goals.

I collaborated with the fractional CMO to sharpen the homepage messaging, redesign the Social product page and build a new case study templates to strengthen trust with prospective customers. I have also recently introduced a Hotjar surveys to gather qualitative feedback directly from users about their needs, segments, and geographies.

## Reflections

While engagement and intent signals have improved significantly, the next focus is on conversion completion. More users are now starting the "Book a demo" form, but a notable proportion drop off before submitting — pointing to an opportunity to investigate form friction, including field requirements, mobile usability, and copy clarity.

Analytics and survey feedback have also surfaced an audience insight worth acting on: while the initial assumption was an enterprise-first customer base, there is growing interest from individual creators and small businesses. This opens up an opportunity to refine positioning, messaging, and case studies to better serve these segments — potentially through dedicated landing pages and tailored value propositions.
